The Yellow-bellied Slider

Description

Adult male yellow-bellied sliders typically reach 5–9 inches in length; females range from 8–13 inches. The carapace (upper shell) is typically brown and black, often with yellow stripes. The skin is olive green with prominent patches of yellow down the neck and legs. As the name implies, the plastron (bottom shell) is mostly yellow with black spots along the edges. Adult males tend to grow darker as they age. Yellow-bellied sliders are often confused with Eastern River Cooters, who also have yellow stripes on the neck and yellow undersides, but the latter lack the green spots characteristic of T. scripta scripta. The yellow belly often has an "s"-shaped yellow stripe on its face.
